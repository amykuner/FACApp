ETHOS

At university, as a woman in STEM, I was very used to my male counterparts speaking over me and assuming they knew better than me, due to their implicit biases. This was infuriating and incredibly disheartening, especially as it happened on an almost daily basis. And yet, I know I am incredibly lucky. I am a woman, but I only had to fight against this one bias. There were many students on my course who did not receive the same level of respect that I did due to their ethnicity or gender identity, and many more who were discouraged from applying to my university because of these factors. I was constantly discouraged from participating in discussions, but I know that I was lucky to have support to overcome this, and to not have experienced this before university. This is why I am so passionate about the inclusion of diversity – so that those who don’t have that level of support feel there is a place for them, even if it doesn’t feel like it in that moment.
